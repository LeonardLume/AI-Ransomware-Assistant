## When to Use
Use this skill when the assessment needs to determine whether plans have been practiced and whether leadership, IT, communications, legal, and vendors understand their roles during a ransomware event.

## What to Ask
- Has the ransomware or incident response plan been practiced?
- Who participated in the last exercise?
- Were business leaders, IT, MSP, communications, legal, or insurance contacts included?
- Were decisions and gaps recorded?
- Were follow-up actions assigned and completed?
- Is there a plan to repeat the exercise?

## Risk Logic
Low risk means tabletop exercises are run periodically, findings are tracked, and actions are completed. Medium risk means exercises happen informally but evidence or follow-up is weak. High risk means the plan has never been practiced with the right people. Critical risk means there is no plan to test and key roles are unknown.

## Recommended Actions
- Run a 60 to 90 minute ransomware tabletop exercise.
- Include leadership, IT or MSP, communications, legal, and business process owners.
- Record decisions, assumptions, and gaps.
- Assign owners and deadlines for exercise findings.
- Update the response plan after the exercise.
- Repeat the exercise at least annually or after major IT changes.

## Evidence Checklist
- Tabletop agenda.
- Participant list.
- Scenario summary.
- Decisions and gaps log.
- Action tracker with owners and deadlines.
- Updated incident response plan.
- Date of next planned exercise.

## Client Explanation
A tabletop exercise is a low-cost practice session. It helps people find gaps before a real crisis, when time is short and normal systems may not be available.
