## When to Use
Use this skill for optional employee and personal security hygiene guidance that supports ransomware readiness without changing the official numeric score.

## What to Ask
- Do employees use a password manager or another control against password reuse?
- Is MFA enabled for employee email and important work services?
- Do employees know how to recognize and report suspicious email or links?
- Are work device operating systems and browsers updated regularly?
- Are MFA recovery codes stored safely?
- Are breach alerts or password reset actions handled through approved channels?

## Risk Logic
Low risk means employees have practical controls for passwords, MFA, phishing reporting, updates, and recovery codes. Medium risk means some controls exist but coverage is inconsistent. High risk means password reuse, weak MFA coverage, outdated devices, or unclear reporting can increase the chance of account compromise. This skill does not calculate numeric score.

## Recommended Actions
- Roll out or standardize password manager use.
- Enable MFA for employee email and important work services.
- Publish a short phishing reporting path.
- Confirm operating system and browser updates are enabled for work devices.
- Store MFA recovery codes in an approved secure location.
- Use legal and approved breach-alert channels; never ask employees to share passwords.

## Evidence Checklist
- Password manager guidance or policy.
- MFA coverage report for employee email and key services.
- Phishing reporting channel or short awareness note.
- Device update report or MSP confirmation.
- Recovery code storage guidance.
- Record of password reset actions after approved breach alerts.

## Client Explanation
Many ransomware incidents start with everyday account or device weaknesses. Simple employee hygiene makes stolen passwords, phishing, and outdated browsers less likely to become a business-wide incident.
